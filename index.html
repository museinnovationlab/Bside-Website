How it works

Three steps. Then it just shows up.

1

Connect your music

Link Spotify, Apple Music, or Last.fm — or just tell us a few artists you love. The more you connect, the sharper it gets.

2

We read the edges of your taste

Not just your top artists — the genres, eras, and adjacent corners that say what you'd reach for next.

3

Get five new albums a week

A fresh radar every week, each pick with a one-line reason. Play it in your app of choice, or export the whole thing as a playlist.

What's inside

Built for finding, not for charts.

Discovery only

We never recommend what you already play. Every pick is something new — and once you've seen it, you'll never see it twice.

Your taste, from every source

Spotify, Apple Music, and Last.fm merge into one profile. Add a new source later and nothing you've built gets lost.

Critics' best-of, ranked for you

Pitchfork, Stereogum, Rolling Stone and more — re-sorted by how well each album fits you, not just where it landed on their list.

Fresh drops you'd actually want

New releases from artists you already love, surfaced the week they land — never a firehose, just yours.

One tap to play

Open any pick in your default player, or turn a week's radar — or any list — into a real playlist you can press play on.

Dial it in

Too safe? Too far out? Tell each pick what you think and the next radar adjusts. It learns the way you actually listen.

Why B-Side

The algorithm feeds you more of the same. We don't.

Streaming got really good at playing you what you already like. B-Side is for the other thing — the dusty crate, the record-store clerk's "oh, you'll love this," the flip side of the single. We do one job: put genuinely new music in front of you, every week, and make it easy to play.

Flip the record. There's a whole other side.

Questions

Good to know

Do I need a paid Spotify or Apple Music account?

You can connect any of Spotify, Apple Music, or Last.fm to shape your taste. To play full albums and export playlists you'll use your own streaming app, so a subscription there helps — but you can browse and discover either way. You can also skip connecting and just pick a few favorite artists to start.

How is this different from Discover Weekly or my streaming app's recommendations?

Those are tuned to keep you listening to what you already like. B-Side is tuned to find what you don't know yet — and it never repeats a pick. It also pulls from critics' lists and new releases, not just one platform's catalog signals.

Will adding a new music service erase my taste profile?

No. Sources are additive — each one merges in without overwriting the others. Connect Last.fm months after Spotify and you keep everything you've built; the picks just get sharper.
